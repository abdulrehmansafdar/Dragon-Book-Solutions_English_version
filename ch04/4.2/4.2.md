### 4.2.4

There is a commonly used extended grammar representation.In this notation, the square brackets and curly braces in the production body are metasymbols and have the following meanings:

1. Square brackets around one or more grammar symbols indicate that these constructs are optional.Therefore, the production A -> X\[Y\]Z has the same effect as the two productions A -> XYZ and A -> XZ.
2. Curly braces around one or more grammar symbols indicate that these symbols may be repeated any number of times (including zero times).Therefore, A -> X{YZ} has the same effect as the following infinite sequence of productions: A -> X, A -> XYZ, A -> XYZYZ, ... and so on.

It is clear that these two extensions do not add grammatical functions.That is, any language generated by a grammar with these extended representations can also be generated by a grammar without extended representations.
